Ethernet Cable Standards

The IEEE 802.3 standard defines several physical implementations that support Ethernet. The figure summarizes the standards of different Ethernet cable types.

1000BASE-T is the most commonly implemented Ethernet architecture today. The name indicates the features of the standard:

  • The 1000 represents a speed of 1000 Mb/s or 1 Gb/s.

  • BASE represents baseband transmission. In baseband transmission, the entire bandwidth of a cable is used for one type of signal.

  • The T represents twisted-pair copper cabling.

The figure on this page is a table listing the following standards of different Ethernet cable types. Standard: 10BASE-T, Media: Category 3, Transfer Rates: Transfers data at a rate of 10 Mb/s. Standard: 100BASE-TX, Media Category: Category 5, Transfer Rates: At 100 Mb/s, transfer rates of 100BASE-TX are ten times that of 10BASE-T. Standard: 1000BASE-T, Media Category: Category 5e, 6, Transfer Rates: The 1000BASE-T architecture supports data transfer rates of 1 Gb/s. Standard: 10GBASE-T, Media Category: Category 6a, 7, Transfer Rates: The 10GBASE-T architecture supports data transfer rates of 10 Gb/s.

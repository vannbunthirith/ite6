Identify the Problem

Printer problems can result from a combination of hardware, software, and connectivity issues. A technician must be able to determine if the problem exists with the device, a cable connection, or the computer to which the printer is connected. Computer technicians must be able to analyze the problem and determine the cause of the error to repair the printer issues.

The first step in the troubleshooting process is to identify the problem. The figure shows a list of open-ended and closed-ended questions to ask the customer.

The figure on this page is a table that lists step 1 in troubleshooting printer problems, identify the problem. Open-ended questions: What problems are you experiencing with your printer or scanner? What software or hardware has been changed recently on your computer? What were you doing when the problem was identified? What error messages have you received? Close-ended Questions: Is the printer under warranty? Can you print a test page? Is this a new printer? Is the printer powered on?
